Atomic clock

The atomic clock is widely recognized as a cutting-edge technology due to its exceptional precision and stability. Its extensive use has significantly impacted the advancement of technology, which we often take for granted. The importance of atomic clocks extends far beyond simple timekeeping, as they serve as a foundational element in numerous technological domains, including Global Positioning Systems (GPS), Telecommunications, Secure Bank Transactions, High-speed Mobile Networks (4G+), Geodesy, Autonomous Cars, and Data Processing.

Quantum Gravimeter

Absolute quantum gravimeter measures the acceleration of a free-falling test mass in a vacuum: the ballistic free-fall of an ensemble of laser-cooled atoms is accurately monitored and the acceleration of gravity is then inferred. Gravimetry is a potential field technique that measures variations in the Earth's gravitational field. Gravimetric surveys are carried out using extremely sensitive instruments capable of measuring tiny variations in the gravitational field.
